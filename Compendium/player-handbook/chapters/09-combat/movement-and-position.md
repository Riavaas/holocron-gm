# Movement and Position

## Source

* Book: SW5e Player's Handbook
* Pages: 222-223
* Original section: Movement and Position

## Summary

Combat movement can be split around actions, affected by different speeds, slowed by terrain, limited by creature space, and changed by prone, flight, squeezing, and grid play.

## Key Rules

* A creature can break movement up before and after actions.
* Different movement speeds can matter when switching movement modes.
* Difficult terrain increases movement cost.
* Creature size and space determine blocking, reach context, and squeezing.
* Being prone changes movement and attack context.

## GM Use

Clarify whether a route is open, blocked, difficult, vertical, or risky before the player commits movement.

## Common Rulings

* Let players split movement unless a trigger or hazard interrupts.
* Use squeezing for vents, maintenance shafts, crowds, and narrow ruins.
* Flying movement should respect hazards, falls, and environment.
