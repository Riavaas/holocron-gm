# Cover

## Source

* Book: SW5e Player's Handbook
* Pages: 226-226
* Original section: Cover

## Summary

Cover protects a target by making attacks or certain effects harder to land. The degree of cover depends on how much of the target is blocked.

## Key Rules

* Cover matters when an obstacle, creature, wall, vehicle, or terrain blocks a line of attack.
* Better cover gives stronger protection.
* Some effects ignore cover or interact with it differently.
* Creatures can provide cover if using that variant.

## GM Use

Call cover before the attack roll when possible. This makes tactical positioning feel fair and visible.

## Common Rulings

* If unsure, judge how much of the target is exposed from the attacker's position.
* Cover is directional; a target may have cover from one attacker and not another.
* Moving to flank or elevate can remove cover.
