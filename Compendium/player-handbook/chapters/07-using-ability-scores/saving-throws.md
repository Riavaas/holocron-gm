# Saving Throws

## Source

* Book: SW5e Player's Handbook
* Pages: 214-214
* Original section: Saving Throws

## Summary

Saving throws resist threats, powers, hazards, poisons, explosions, and other harmful effects. The effect usually says which ability save is required and what happens on success or failure.

## Key Rules

* Roll d20 + relevant ability modifier + proficiency if proficient in that saving throw.
* The DC comes from the effect, caster, monster, trap, or GM ruling.
* Success often reduces or avoids harm; failure applies the full consequence.

## GM Use

Use saves for reactive danger. Use checks for proactive attempts. If the character is resisting something happening to them, it is usually a save.

## Common Rulings

* Dexterity saves avoid blasts and sudden hazards.
* Constitution saves resist poison, pressure, exhaustion, and bodily strain.
* Wisdom or Charisma saves resist mental, Force, or willpower effects.
