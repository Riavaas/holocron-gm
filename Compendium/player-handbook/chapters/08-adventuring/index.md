# Adventuring

## Source

* Book: SW5e Player's Handbook
* Pages: 215-220
* Original section: Chapter 8: Adventuring

## Summary

This chapter covers the non-combat backbone of sessions: time, travel, movement, terrain, environmental hazards, vision, food and water, social interaction, resting, and downtime.

## Key Rules

* Time scale changes by activity: moments, minutes, hours, days, and downtime.
* Movement depends on speed, terrain, travel pace, and special movement.
* Vision and light determine what characters can perceive.
* Resting restores resources under short-rest and long-rest rules.
* Social interaction is resolved through roleplay, approach, and ability checks when needed.

## GM Use

Use this chapter for exploration pacing. It helps decide how far the party gets, what they notice, when resources matter, and when rest is possible.

## Common Rulings

* If a travel choice creates risk, set pace and ask who is watching, navigating, tracking, or hiding.
* If the environment matters, make terrain, light, food, and time visible to players.
* If a social scene stalls, ask for approach and stakes before calling for a check.
