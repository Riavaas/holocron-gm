# Making an Attack

## Source

* Book: SW5e Player's Handbook
* Pages: 225-226
* Original section: Making an Attack

## Summary

Attacks use a d20 roll plus modifiers against Armor Class. The chapter covers attack modifiers, natural 1 or 20 outcomes, unseen attackers, melee attacks, opportunity attacks, grappling, shoving, ranged attacks, and fighting with multiple weapons.

## Key Rules

* Pick a target, determine modifiers, roll the attack, then resolve hit or miss.
* Melee and ranged attacks use different positioning risks.
* Opportunity attacks punish certain movement out of reach.
* Grappling, shoving, and tripping use contest-style combat procedures.
* Ranged attacks are affected by range and nearby threats.

## GM Use

Before the roll, confirm target, range, visibility, cover, and whether the attacker is proficient or advantaged/disadvantaged.

## Common Rulings

* If the target cannot be clearly perceived, apply unseen-target logic.
* If an enemy leaves reach, check for opportunity attack triggers.
* If a player wants a stunt, route it to grapple, shove, trip, contest, or improvised action.
