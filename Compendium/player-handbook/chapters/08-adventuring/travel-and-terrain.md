# Travel and Terrain

## Source

* Book: SW5e Player's Handbook
* Pages: 216-217
* Original section: Difficult Terrain, Special Types of Movement, Activity While Traveling

## Summary

Terrain and travel activities convert a simple movement declaration into meaningful choices. Obstacles, stealth, marching order, hazards, and special movement determine cost and risk.

## Key Rules

* Difficult terrain slows movement.
* Climbing, swimming, crawling, and jumping may require extra movement or checks.
* Stealth during travel depends on pace and cover.
* Noticing threats can use active or passive checks depending on the scene.

## GM Use

Use terrain to make locations matter. A ruined Jedi enclave, swamp, cliff, maintenance shaft, or flooded corridor should change movement choices.

## Common Rulings

* Ask for checks only when failure changes the scene.
* If travel is dangerous, combine pace, terrain, and passive checks.
* Let equipment and clever plans reduce risk before rolling.
