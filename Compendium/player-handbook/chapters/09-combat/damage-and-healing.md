# Damage and Healing

## Source

* Book: SW5e Player's Handbook
* Pages: 227-229
* Original section: Damage and Healing

## Summary

Damage reduces hit points; healing restores them without exceeding the maximum unless a rule says otherwise. Critical hits, damage types, resistance, vulnerability, and hit dice affect the result.

## Key Rules

* Damage rolls determine hit point loss.
* Critical hits increase damage dice as specified by the combat rules.
* Damage type matters when resistance, vulnerability, or immunity applies.
* Healing restores hit points but usually cannot exceed maximum hit points.
* Hit dice are a recovery resource.
* Temporary hit points and temporary Force or tech points are tracked separately from normal pools.

## GM Use

Resolve damage in this order: roll damage, identify type, apply critical hit rules, apply resistance/vulnerability/immunity, subtract from hit points, then check 0 HP rules.

## Common Rulings

* Ask for damage type when it matters.
* Do not apply resistance twice unless a rule says to.
* Healing a creature at 0 HP changes the scene immediately.
