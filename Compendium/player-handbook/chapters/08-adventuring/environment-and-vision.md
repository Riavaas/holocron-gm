# Environment and Vision

## Source

* Book: SW5e Player's Handbook
* Pages: 217-218
* Original section: The Environment, Vision and Light

## Summary

Environmental rules cover hazards such as falling, suffocation, pressure, darkness, and visibility. Vision determines what creatures can notice and target.

## Key Rules

* Falling and suffocation create direct risk and often require quick adjudication.
* Light and darkness affect perception, stealth, and targeting.
* Special senses such as blindsight, darkvision, and truesight change what a creature can perceive.
* Pressure or hostile atmospheres can turn travel into an endurance problem.

## GM Use

Name the visibility and environmental pressure early. Players make better choices when they know whether the scene is dark, smoky, flooded, unstable, or airless.

## Common Rulings

* Darkness should affect both navigation and combat targeting.
* Use passive checks for unnoticed environmental clues.
* If the hazard is obvious but unavoidable, focus on saves, resources, and time.
