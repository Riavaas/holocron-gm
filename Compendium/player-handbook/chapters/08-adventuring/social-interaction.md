# Social Interaction

## Source

* Book: SW5e Player's Handbook
* Pages: 218-219
* Original section: Social Interaction, Roleplaying

## Summary

Social interaction combines player approach, character intent, NPC attitude, and ability checks when the result is uncertain. Roleplay can be descriptive or active.

## Key Rules

* The player states what the character tries to accomplish.
* The GM considers NPC goals, attitude, risk, and leverage.
* Ability checks can resolve uncertain persuasion, deception, intimidation, insight, or performance.
* Results should change the conversation, even on failure.

## GM Use

Decide what the NPC wants before the roll. This keeps social scenes from becoming a single Charisma check detached from motives.

## Common Rulings

* Good leverage can lower the DC or remove the need to roll.
* Bad leverage or impossible requests can fail without a roll.
* A failed social check can close one path, raise cost, or reveal a complication.
