# Time and Movement

## Source

* Book: SW5e Player's Handbook
* Pages: 215-216
* Original section: Time, Movement, Speed, Travel Pace

## Summary

Adventuring time shifts between scene time, travel time, and downtime. Movement begins with a character's speed, then changes through pace, terrain, marching order, stealth, and special movement.

## Key Rules

* Speed defines tactical movement and short-distance movement.
* Travel pace controls distance, awareness, stealth, and urgency.
* Marching order matters when threats, traps, or visibility depend on position.
* Characters can perform activities while traveling if the activity fits the pace and situation.

## GM Use

Use pace and marching order before exploration gets dangerous. They create fast rulings for ambushes, scouting, traps, and pursuit.

## Common Rulings

* Fast pace trades caution for speed.
* Slow pace supports stealth and careful searching.
* If the group splits travel roles, ask who leads, watches, maps, tracks, or searches.
